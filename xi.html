4. Yala National Park

Yala () National Park is Sri Lanka's second largest and most popular national park, located near the Indian Ocean. Three of the five blocks in the park are now accessible to the public. Kumana National Park (Yala East) and Lunugamvehera National Park are both located in close proximity to each other. Palatupana (Block 1) is one of the blocks with individual names The Southern Province and Uva Province form the southeastern region of the country where it is located. About 300 kilometers (190 mi) from Colombo, the park covers 979 square kilometers (378 sq mi). The first two designated national parks in Sri Lanka were established in 1900 with the designation of Yala as a wildlife sanctuary and Wilpattu as the first two. The park is widely recognized for its diverse wildlife and plays a critical role in protecting Sri Lankan elephants, leopards, and aquatic birds.

3. Bentota Beach

Bentota is a coastal town in Sri Lanka, located in the Galle District of the Southern Province. It is approximately 65 kilometres (40 mi) south of Colombo and 56 kilometres (35 mi) north of Galle. Bentota is situated on the southern bank of the Bentota River mouth, at an elevation of 3 metres (9.8 ft) above the sea level. The Bentota Beach is a wonder for tourists. A tropical climate with high temperates throughout the year, but a natural sight for everyone.
